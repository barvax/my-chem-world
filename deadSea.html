History & heritage around the Dead Sea

The Dead Sea region has been a crossroads of trade, culture, and ancient kingdoms for thousands of years. Natural minerals, salts, and bitumen from the area were historically valued in commerce and daily life.

Ancient routes & trade Caravans and settlements developed around water sources and trade corridors in the broader region.
Classical-era references Writers in antiquity noted the area’s unusual waters and materials, contributing to its enduring mystique.
Modern wellness culture Today, mineral bathing and spa traditions are globally popular, and the Dead Sea remains an iconic source.

FAQ

Quick answers before you buy.

How do I use it in a bath?

Add 1–2 cups to warm water, stir to dissolve, and soak for 15–20 minutes. Rinse after use and moisturize. If you have sensitive skin, start with a smaller amount.

Can I use it as a body scrub?

Yes—mix a small amount with a carrier oil or gentle cleanser and massage lightly. Avoid broken or freshly shaved skin.

Is it food-grade?

This product is intended for bath & body use only, not for consumption.

Any precautions?

Avoid contact with eyes. Do not use on broken skin. Discontinue if irritation occurs. If pregnant, nursing, or under medical care, consult a professional.
